Engineering Mathematics: Complex Numbers Test

Questions & Detailed Answers

Questions

1. Quadratic Solutions

What are the two solutions to the quadratic equation $x^2 + 81 = 0$?

2. Real & Imaginary Parts

Identify the real and imaginary parts of the complex number $z = \frac{3}{4} - j\sqrt{5}$.

3. Powers of j (Positive)

Simplify the expression $j^{43}$.

4. Powers of j (Negative)

Evaluate $j^{-18}$.

5. Powers of j (Operations)

What is the result of $j^{100} - j^{102}$?

6. Complex Addition

Given $z_1 = -4 + j7$ and $z_2 = 5 - j2$, find the sum $z_1 + z_2$.

7. Complex Subtraction

Using the same $z_1$ and $z_2$, calculate the difference $z_2 - z_1$.

8. Complex Multiplication

Find the product of $(2 - j5)$ and $(3 + j4)$.

9. Complex Conjugate

What is the complex conjugate of $z = -8 + j$?

10. Conjugate Product

Calculate the product of $(3 - j\sqrt{2})$ and its complex conjugate.

11. Complex Division

Perform the division $\frac{2 - j3}{4 + j}$.

12. Squaring a Complex Number

Simplify the expression $(1+j)^2$.

13. Evaluating Expressions

If $z = 2+j$, evaluate $z^2 - 2z + 3$.

14. Solving Quadratic Equations

Solve the quadratic equation $2x^2 + 5x + 4 = 0$.

15. Equating Complex Numbers

Find the real values of `x` and `y` that satisfy the equation: $(x + jy)(3 - j4) = 17 - j6$.

16. Solving for Variables

If $(a+b) + j(a-b) = (2+j5)^2$, find the values of `a` and `b`.

17. Argand Diagram Plotting

Plot the complex number $z = -4 - j3$ on an Argand diagram.

18. Graphical Addition

Given $z_1 = 3+j$ and $z_2 = 1+j4$, graphically represent the sum $z_1 + z_2$ using the parallelogram law on an Argand diagram. What is the resulting complex number?

19. Modulus and Argument

Determine the modulus (r) and the argument ($\theta$ in degrees) of the complex number $z = \sqrt{3} - j$.

20. Polar Form Conversion

Express the complex number $z = -7 + j7$ in polar form, $r(\cos\theta + j\sin\theta)$.

21. Shorthand Polar Form

Express $z = 5 - j12$ in the shorthand polar form $r\angle\theta$.

22. Rectangular Form Conversion

Convert the complex number $z = 4(\cos(150^\circ) + j\sin(150^\circ))$ from polar form to rectangular form ($a+jb$).

23. Shorthand to Rectangular

A complex number is given in polar form as $8\angle-60^\circ$. Express it in rectangular form.

24. Modulus/Argument of a Fraction

Find the modulus and argument of $z = \frac{1}{-1-j}$.

25. Polar Multiplication

If $z_1 = 10\angle30^\circ$ and $z_2 = 2\angle-45^\circ$, find the product $z_1z_2$ in polar form.

Answers

1. Solution

$x = \pm j9$

2. Solution

Real Part: $\frac{3}{4}$, Imaginary Part: $-\sqrt{5}$

3. Solution

$j^{43} = (j^4)^{10} \cdot j^3 = 1^{10} \cdot (-j) = -j$

4. Solution

$j^{-18} = (j^{-4})^4 \cdot j^{-2} = 1^4 \cdot (-1) = -1$

5. Solution

$j^{100} = (j^4)^{25} = 1$. $j^{102} = (j^4)^{25} \cdot j^2 = -1$.

$1 - (-1) = 2$

6. Solution

$(-4+5) + j(7-2) = 1 + j5$

7. Solution

$(5 - (-4)) + j(-2 - 7) = 9 - j9$

8. Solution

$(6 - (-20)) + j(8 - 15) = 26 - j7$

9. Solution

The conjugate is $-8 - j$.

10. Solution

$(3 - j\sqrt{2})(3 + j\sqrt{2}) = 3^2 + (\sqrt{2})^2 = 9 + 2 = 11$

11. Solution

$\frac{(2 - j3)(4 - j)}{(4 + j)(4 - j)} = \frac{8 - 2j - 12j + 3j^2}{16 - j^2} = \frac{5 - 14j}{17} = \frac{5}{17} - j\frac{14}{17}$

12. Solution

$1 + 2j + j^2 = 1 + 2j - 1 = 2j$

13. Solution

$(2+j)^2 - 2(2+j) + 3 = (4 + 4j - 1) - (4 + 2j) + 3 = (3 + 4j) - 4 - 2j + 3 = 2 + 2j$

14. Solution

$x = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{5^2 - 4(2)(4)}}{2(2)} = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{25 - 32}}{4} = -\frac{5}{4} \pm j\frac{\sqrt{7}}{4}$

15. Solution

$(3x+4y) + j(3y-4x) = 17 - j6$.

$3x+4y=17$ and $3y-4x=-6$. Solving simultaneously gives $x=1, y=2$.

16. Solution

$(2+j5)^2 = 4 + 20j - 25 = -21 + j20$.

$a+b=-21$ and $a-b=20$. Solving simultaneously gives $a=-0.5, b=-20.5$.

17. Solution

A point plotted at coordinates (-4, -3) on the complex plane.

18. Solution

The resulting complex number is $4+j5$. The graph shows vectors from the origin to (3,1) and (1,4), with the resultant vector being the diagonal of the parallelogram ending at (4,5).

19. Solution

$r = \sqrt{(\sqrt{3})^2 + (-1)^2} = \sqrt{3+1} = 2$.

$\theta = \tan^{-1}(\frac{-1}{\sqrt{3}}) = -30^\circ$ or $330^\circ$.

20. Solution

$r = \sqrt{(-7)^2 + 7^2} = 7\sqrt{2}$. $\theta = \tan^{-1}(\frac{7}{-7}) = 135^\circ$.

$z = 7\sqrt{2}(\cos(135^\circ) + j\sin(135^\circ))$

21. Solution

$r = \sqrt{5^2 + (-12)^2} = 13$. $\theta = \tan^{-1}(\frac{-12}{5}) \approx -67.38^\circ$.

$13\angle-67.38^\circ$

22. Solution

$a = 4\cos(150^\circ) = 4(-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}) = -2\sqrt{3}$.

$b = 4\sin(150^\circ) = 4(\frac{1}{2}) = 2$. Result: $-2\sqrt{3} + j2$.

23. Solution

$a = 8\cos(-60^\circ) = 4$. $b = 8\sin(-60^\circ) = -4\sqrt{3}$.

Result: $4 - j4\sqrt{3}$

24. Solution

$z = \frac{1}{-1-j} \cdot \frac{-1+j}{-1+j} = \frac{-1+j}{2} = -0.5 + j0.5$.

$r = \sqrt{(-0.5)^2 + 0.5^2} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$. $\theta = 135^\circ$.

25. Solution

$r = 10 \times 2 = 20$. $\theta = 30^\circ + (-45^\circ) = -15^\circ$.

Result: $20\angle-15^\circ$
