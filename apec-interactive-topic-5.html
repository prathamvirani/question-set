Nation-State Formation in the Malaysian Context

Malaysia's unique journey is as a state with a multi-ethnic population working towards a unified national identity.

Key Elements of Integration

Political Hegemony: Recognition and acceptance of both indigenous peoples (Bumiputera) and migrant communities as citizens.
The Constitution: Establishes core national principles like the role of the Malay Rulers, Islam, the Malay language, and the special position of Bumiputera (Article 153), while also protecting the rights of other communities.
Integration of Minorities: The King serves as a symbol of unity, and the constitution guarantees freedom of religion and the use of other languages.
Formation of Malaysia: The inclusion of Sabah and Sarawak recognized their people as Bumiputera, making them a foundational part of the nation.
