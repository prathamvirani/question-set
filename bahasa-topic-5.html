Bab 5: Percutian (Vacations)

A guide to essential language for travelling, including grammar, directions, and useful phrases.

1. Pengenalan (Introduction)

This chapter focuses on vacations, a popular activity for international students. To communicate effectively while on holiday in Malaysia, it's helpful to understand some basic language components like:

  • Imbuhan: Affixes that change a word's meaning.
  • Kata Tanya: Question words (who, what, where).
  • Kata Arah: Directional words (left, right, straight).
  • Penanda Wacana: Discourse markers to connect ideas (first, then, finally).

2. Soalan Berkaitan Pelancongan (Tourism Questions)

Here are some common questions you might ask when planning a trip.

Bahasa Melayu English Translation
Apakah tempat yang paling menarik di Malaysia? Which is the most interesting place in Malaysia?
Apakah keistimewaan tempat itu? What is special about that place?
Di manakah hotel bajet di sini? Where are the budget hotels here?
Berapakah harga pakej untuk pergi ke Sarawak? How much is the package to go to Sarawak?

3. Tatabahasa: Imbuhan `meN-` (The `meN-` Prefix)

The prefix `meN-` is attached to a base word to form an active verb. Its spelling changes based on the first letter of the base word.

Becomes `me-`

For base words starting with:
l, m, n, ng, ny, r, w, y

e.g., `lawat` → `melawat` (to visit)

Becomes `mem-`

For base words starting with:
b, f, v and loanwords with p

e.g., `buat` → `membuat` (to do)

Becomes `men-`

For base words starting with:
c, d, j, sy, z and loanwords with t, s

e.g., `dengar` → `mendengar` (to listen)

Becomes `meng-`

For base words starting with vowels (a, e, i, o, u) and g, h, kh

e.g., `ambil` → `mengambil` (to take)

Becomes `menge-`

For single-syllable words.

e.g., `cat` → `mengecat` (to paint)

Special Rule (p, t, k, s)

The first letter is dropped and replaced.

  • `p` → `m` (pakai → memakai)
  • `t` → `n` (tulis → menulis)
  • `k` → `ng` (kumpul → mengumpul)
  • `s` → `ny` (sapu → menyapu)

4. Arah & Perjalanan (Directions & Travelling)

Essential words and phrases for navigating your way around.

Kata Arah (Directional Words)
Di depanIn front of
Di belakangBehind
Ke kiri / Ke kananTurn left / Turn right
TerusGo straight
SeberangAcross / Opposite

Penanda Wacana (Discourse Markers)

  • Mula-mula - First
  • Kemudian - Then
  • Selepas itu - After that
  • Seterusnya - Next
  • Akhirnya - Finally

Example of Giving Directions:

Mula-mula, keluar dari stesen kereta api dan terus berjalan ke hadapan.

Kemudian, belok kiri di persimpangan pertama.

Selepas itu, teruskan berjalan selama 5 minit.

Seterusnya, lintasi jalan dan berjalan ke arah taman.

Akhirnya, anda akan menemui muzium di seberang taman.
