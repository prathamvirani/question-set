Key Concepts in Ethnic Relations

Explore the different ways ethnic groups interact within a diverse society. Click on a concept to learn more.

Segregation

The enforced separation of different ethnic groups in a country, community, or institution, either physically or socially.

Accommodation

A process where different ethnic groups accept and respect each other's cultures, allowing them to coexist peacefully without losing their distinct identities.

Acculturation

The process of borrowing and adapting cultural elements from other ethnic groups, leading to a blend of traditions while maintaining core identities.

Assimilation

A process where a minority group or culture comes to resemble a society's majority group by taking on its values, behaviors, and beliefs.

Amalgamation

The biological merging of distinct ethnic groups through intermarriage, creating a new, blended culture and society over time.

The Straits of Malacca: A Court of Diversity

Historically, the Straits of Malacca was more than a trade route; it was a vibrant hub where civilizations met. This "court of diversity" brought together Malays, Chinese, Indians, Arabs, and Europeans, creating a unique melting pot. The Malacca Sultanate, in particular, stands as a testament to a thriving multicultural society where different races and religions coexisted in harmony.
