Bahasa Melayu: Sounds & Pronunciation

Topic 1.1: A detailed guide to Vowels, Diphthongs, and Consonants.

1. Vokal (Vowels)

Bahasa Melayu has six vowel sounds. The letter 'e' is special because it has two distinct pronunciations: **e (taling)** and **e (pepet)**.

Vokal /a/

Sounds like the 'a' in "father".

  • Mata (Eye)
  • Bapa (Father)
  • Saya (I/Me)

Vokal /i/

Sounds like the 'ee' in "feet".

  • Cili (Chili)
  • Gigi (Teeth)

Vokal /o/

Sounds like the 'o' in "note".

  • Bola (Ball)
  • Sotong (Squid)

Vokal /u/

Sounds like the 'oo' in "food".

  • Susu (Milk)
  • Pintu (Door)

Vokal /e/ (taling)

Sounds like the 'e' in "set".

  • Epal (Apple)
  • Meja (Table)

Vokal /e/ (pepet)

Sounds like the 'a' in "again".

  • Telur (Egg)
  • Empat (Four)

2. Diftong (Diphthongs)

A diphthong is a sound formed by combining two vowels in a single syllable. There are three main diphthongs in Malay.

Diftong /ai/

  • Kedai (Shop)
  • Sungai (River)
  • Ramai (Many)

Diftong /au/

  • Pulau (Island)
  • Hijau (Green)
  • Kalau (If)

Diftong /oi/

  • Koboi (Cowboy)
  • Dodoi (Lullaby)
  • Amboi (Oh!)

3. Konsonan & Gugusan Konsonan (Consonants & Clusters)

A consonant cluster is a group of two or more consonants appearing together. Here are some common clusters in Malay.

Cluster Example Word English Meaning
gh Ghairah Passionate
kh Khas Special
ng Ngeri Horrifying
ny Nyanyi Sing
sy Syarat Condition
