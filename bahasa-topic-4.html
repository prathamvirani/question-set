Bahasa Melayu: Conversational Situations

Topic 4: Mastering conversations on the phone, at counters, and in shops.

1. Perbualan di Telefon (Telephone Conversations)

Essential phrases for handling phone calls politely and effectively.

Greetings & Openers

  • Apa khabar? (How are you?)
  • Siapakah dalam talian? (Who is on the line?)
  • Adakah ini...? (Is this...?)

Handling Issues

  • Maaf, tolong ulang. (Sorry, please repeat.)
  • Talian sibuk. (The line is busy.)
  • Sila menelefon semula. (Please call again later.)

Managing the Call

  • Tunggu sebentar. (Please hold on.)
  • Dia tidak ada. (He/She is not here.)
  • Saya akan telefon semula. (I will call back.)

Kata Rujukan Hormat (Honorific Terms)

Always use respectful terms when addressing others, especially those who are older or in a position of authority.

Encik (Mr.) Puan (Mrs.) Cik (Miss) Profesor Cikgu (Teacher) Pak cik (Uncle) Mak cik (Auntie)

2. Perbualan di Kaunter (At a Counter)

A typical interaction when buying tickets or seeking services.

Example: Buying a Bus Ticket

Ali: Selamat pagi. Saya mahu membeli tiket bas ke KLIA.

Kerani: Selamat pagi. Ya, ada. Bilakah awak mahu pergi?

Ali: Esok. Untuk dua orang. Pukul berapa bas ada?

Kerani: Ada pukul 8.00 pagi dan 5.00 petang.

Grammar Focus

Kata Hubung (Conjunctions)

  • dan (and): Saya mahu setem dan sampul surat.
  • tetapi (but): Tempat itu dekat tetapi mahal.
  • atau (or): Awak mahu kopi atau teh?
  • kerana (because): Saya tidak datang kerana sakit.

Kata Bantu (Modals)

  • mahu/hendak (want): Dia mahu membeli tiket.
  • mesti (must): Awak mesti bayar sekarang.
  • boleh (can/may): Boleh saya bantu?
  • harus/patut (should): Kita harus pergi awal.

3. Perbualan di Kedai & Restoran (At a Shop & Restaurant)

Useful descriptive words for ordering food or shopping.

Word Meaning Example Sentence
agak quite Harga ini agak murah. (This price is quite cheap.)
lebih more Saya mahu sup lebih pedas. (I want the soup to be more spicy.)
terlalu too / extremely Kopi ini terlalu pahit. (This coffee is too bitter.)
sangat very Nasi lemak itu sangat pedas. (The nasi lemak is very spicy.)
sebelum/selepas before/after Basuh tangan sebelum makan. (Wash hands before eating.)
