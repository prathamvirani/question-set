ENG1013: Sequences Examination

Questions & Detailed Answers

Questions

Section A: Arithmetic Sequences

1. The 7th term of an arithmetic sequence is 31 and the 15th term is 63. Determine the first term and the common difference.

2. Find the sum of the first 25 terms of the arithmetic sequence: -5, -1, 3, 7, ...

3. A civil engineer is designing a staircase with 40 steps. The first step requires 2 meters of material, and each subsequent step requires 0.15 meters more material than the previous one. Calculate the total length of material required for the entire staircase.

4. The sum of three consecutive terms in an arithmetic progression is 36, and their product is 1428. Find the three terms.

Section B: Geometric Sequences

5. Determine the 10th term of the geometric sequence: 1/16, 1/8, 1/4, 1/2, ...

6. The 4th term of a geometric sequence is 24 and the 7th term is 192. Find the common ratio and the first term.

7. Find the sum of the first 9 terms of the sequence: 128, 64, 32, 16, ...

8. A high-performance pump removes 20% of the remaining air from a container with each stroke. What percentage of the original air is left in the container after 6 strokes?

9. Calculate the sum to infinity of the sequence: 9, -3, 1, -1/3, ...

Section C: Other Sequence Types & Recursion

10. The 3rd and 7th terms of a harmonic sequence are 1/11 and 1/23 respectively. What is the 10th term of this sequence?

11. A sequence is defined by the recursive formula $f(n+1) = f(n) + 9$, with the initial condition $f(1) = 5$. Find the explicit formula for $f(n)$.

12. Given the sequence $f(n) = 5 \cdot (1/2)^n$ for $n \ge 1$, derive its recursive prescription.

Section D: Convergence and Divergence

13. Determine if the sequence $f(n) = \frac{4n^2 - 3n}{2n^2 + 5}$ is convergent or divergent. If it is convergent, find its limit.

14. Find the limit of the sequence $f(n) = \frac{n^3 + 2n - 1}{8n^3 - 5n^2}$.

15. Analyze the sequence $f(n) = n \cdot \sin(\frac{1}{n})$. Is it convergent or divergent? If convergent, what is its limit?

16. Evaluate the limit: $\lim_{n\to\infty} (\sqrt{n^2 + 6n} - n)$.

Section E: Applications and Mixed Problems

17. The first three terms of a sequence are 4, y, 36. Find all possible values of y if the sequence is (a) arithmetic and (b) geometric.

18. A radioactive isotope has a half-life of 10 years. If you start with a 200g sample, how much of the substance will remain after 50 years?

19. An employee starts with an annual salary of RM 45,000. They receive a raise of RM 3,000 at the end of each year. How much total money will they have earned after working for 10 years?

20. The sum of the first n terms of a sequence is given by the formula $S_n = 2n^2 + 5n$. Prove that this sequence is an arithmetic progression and find its common difference.

21. Explain why a harmonic sequence in which all terms are positive must be convergent. What is its limit?

22. Under what condition on the common ratio 'r' is a geometric sequence convergent? What is the limit in this case?

23. Three numbers form a geometric progression. If the second term is increased by 8, the resulting numbers form an arithmetic progression. If the last term of this new progression is increased by 64, the numbers again form a geometric progression. Find the original three numbers.

24. A loan of RM 10,000 is repaid in yearly installments of RM 1,000, with an interest rate of 5% per annum on the outstanding balance. How many years will it take to repay the loan?

25. Compare and contrast the behavior of the sequences $f(n) = n^2$ and $g(n) = 2^n$ as n approaches infinity. Which one grows faster?

1. Solution

$a_7 = a + 6d = 31$; $a_{15} = a + 14d = 63$. Subtracting gives $8d = 32 \implies d = 4$. Substituting back: $a + 6(4) = 31 \implies a = 7$. First term is 7, common difference is 4.

2. Solution

$a = -5, d = 4, n = 25$. $S_{25} = \frac{25}{2}[2(-5) + (24)4] = 12.5[-10 + 96] = 12.5(86) = \textbf{1075}$.

3. Solution

$a = 2, d = 0.15, n = 40$. $S_{40} = \frac{40}{2}[2(2) + (39)0.15] = 20[4 + 5.85] = 20(9.85) = \textbf{197 meters}$.

4. Solution

Let the terms be $x-d, x, x+d$. Sum: $3x = 36 \implies x = 12$. Product: $(12-d)(12)(12+d) = 1428 \implies 144 - d^2 = 119 \implies d^2 = 25 \implies d = \pm 5$. The terms are 7, 12, 17.

5. Solution

6. Solution

$a_4 = ar^3 = 24$; $a_7 = ar^6 = 192$. Dividing gives $r^3 = 8 \implies r = 2$. Substituting back: $a(2)^3 = 24 \implies 8a = 24 \implies a = 3$. Common ratio is 2, first term is 3.

7. Solution

$a = 128, r = 1/2, n = 9$. $S_9 = \frac{128(1 - (1/2)^9)}{1 - 1/2} = 256(1 - 1/512) = 256(\frac{511}{512}) = \textbf{255.5}$.

8. Solution

9. Solution

10. Solution

11. Solution

12. Solution

$f(n+1) = 5 \cdot (1/2)^{n+1} = (5 \cdot (1/2)^n) \cdot (1/2) = \frac{1}{2}f(n)$. Recursive prescription: $f(n+1) = \frac{1}{2}f(n)$ with $f(1) = 5/2$.

15. Solution

As $n \to \infty$, $1/n \to 0$. For small $x$, $\sin(x) \approx x$. So, $\lim_{n\to\infty} n \cdot (\frac{1}{n}) = 1$. Convergent, limit is 1.

16. Solution

Multiply by conjugate: $\lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{(n^2+6n) - n^2}{\sqrt{n^2+6n} + n} = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{6n}{n\sqrt{1+6/n} + n} = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{6}{\sqrt{1+6/n} + 1} = \frac{6}{2} = \textbf{3}$.
