Concept of Ethics and Civilization

An interactive exploration of the historical movements and core ideas that define ethics and civilization in Malaysia's pluralistic society.

Tracing the Movement of Ideas and Practices

Malaysia's identity is a rich blend of diverse historical influences. Click on each section to expand and explore the key civilizational threads.

The journey began in the **Pre-Historic Era** with nomadic settlements, evolving through the **Proto-Historical Period** with kingdoms like Srivijaya. The **Malacca Sultanate** was a pivotal moment, becoming a trade hub that fostered a pluralistic civilization. The arrival of European powers during the **Modern History** period introduced new dynamics and influences.

Indian Civilization

Spread Hinduism and Buddhism, influencing governance, law, language (Sanskrit), and architecture.

Chinese Civilization

Established trade relations and influenced administration, philosophy, and culture, leading to the Baba Nyonya community.

Middle Eastern Civilization

The spread of Islam transformed religious beliefs, governance, and social structures from the 7th century onwards.

Western Civilization

British influence was most profound, impacting government systems, science, and introducing ideologies like secularism.

The modern "borderless world," driven by ICT and social media, has created a **hybrid global civilization**. This ongoing process continues to blend diverse influences from both the East and the West, constantly shaping and reshaping Malaysian identity.

The Concept of Ethics

Ethics in Malaysia is a blend of European thought, religious perspectives, and the colonial experience.

History of European Thought

Before Enlightenment

The **Dark Ages** were dominated by church control, which gave way to the **Renaissance**, a "rebirth" of intellectual inquiry focused on human reason.

During Enlightenment

Known as the **Age of Reason**, this period emphasized knowledge through experience and observation, leading to the rise of **secularism**.

After Enlightenment

The **French and Industrial Revolutions** cemented concepts like individualism, human rights, democracy, and capitalism as central tenets of Western ethics.

The Concept of Civilization

Civilization is shaped by historical forces, cultural values, and religious beliefs. In Malaysia, it's defined by the legacy of colonialism and the East-West dynamic.

Colonialism & Post-Colonialism

British colonialism introduced a **secularist framework** and ideologies like materialism. This has resulted in a layered, **hybrid civilization** in post-colonial Malaysia, blending diverse influences with global trends.

East and West

**Eastern civilization** is largely built on religious and spiritual foundations. In contrast, **Western civilization** is rooted in rationalism, secularism, and scientific advancement, especially after the Enlightenment.

Role of Religion and Beliefs

Religion is a cornerstone of civilization. It provides a foundation for moral values, guides societal development, reflects cultural heritage, and serves as a powerful symbol of unity and national spirit.
