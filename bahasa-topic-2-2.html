Bahasa Melayu: Myself, Friends & Family

Topic 2.1: A guide to introductions, greetings, and basic grammar.

1. Ucapan Tegur (General Greetings)

Basic interactions in Malay start with these common greetings and expressions.

Selamat pagi: Good morning
Selamat tengah hari: Good afternoon
Selamat petang: Good evening
Selamat malam: Good night
Apa khabar?: How are you?
Terima kasih: Thank you
Sama-sama: You're welcome
Jumpa lagi: See you again

2. Time-Related Vocabulary

Days, Weeks, etc.

  • Hari ini: Today
  • Semalam: Yesterday
  • Esok: Tomorrow
  • Minggu depan: Next week
  • Bulan lepas: Last month

Tense & Frequency

  • Akan: Will (Future)
  • Sudah / Telah: Already (Past)
  • Sedang: Doing now
  • Selalu: Always
  • Kadang-kadang: Sometimes

3. Memperkenalkan Diri (Introducing Yourself)

Use these phrases to share information about yourself.

Nama (Name)

"Nama saya ialah Sarah."

Umur (Age)

"Umur saya 20 tahun."

Negara (Country)

"Saya dari Iran."

Hobi (Hobby)

"Hobi saya membaca." (My hobby is reading)

4. Asking Questions

  • What is your name?

    Siapakah nama awak?

  • Where are you from?

    Awak dari mana?

  • How old are you?

    Berapakah umur awak?

5. Tatabahasa (Grammar Focus)

A. Kata Nama (Nouns)

Nouns name a person, place, thing, or idea. They are split into Common Nouns (general) and Proper Nouns (specific).

Kata Nama Am (Common)

e.g., guru (teacher), sekolah (school)

Kata Nama Khas (Proper)

e.g., Nurul, Sunway University

B. Kata Ganti Nama (Pronouns)

Pronouns replace nouns. Personal pronouns are crucial for conversation.

Person Singular Plural
First Person saya, aku kita, kami
Second Person awak, anda, kamu awak semua
Third Person dia, ia, beliau mereka

Note: kita is inclusive (we, including you), while kami is exclusive (we, not including you).
