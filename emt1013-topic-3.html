Engineering Mathematics: Functions II Test

Questions & Detailed Answers

Questions

1. Rational Functions

Find all vertical and horizontal asymptotes for the function: $f(x) = \frac{3x^2 - 12}{x^2 - x - 6}$

2. Rational Functions

Determine the vertical and horizontal asymptotes of the function: $g(x) = \frac{2x+1}{x^2-4}$

3. Rational Functions

Analyze the function $h(x) = \frac{x^3 + 1}{x^2 - 1}$. Does it have a horizontal asymptote? If not, describe the end behavior of the function.

4. Rational Functions

A chemical reaction's rate $R$ (in moles per second) is modeled by $R(C) = \frac{10C}{0.5 + C}$, where $C$ is the concentration of a catalyst. What concentration $C$ results in a reaction rate of 15 moles per second? What is the limiting reaction rate as the concentration of the catalyst becomes very large?

5. Rational Functions

Sketch the graph of the function $f(x) = \frac{x-2}{x+3}$, clearly labeling any asymptotes and intercepts.

6. Circular Functions

Solve the equation $2\sin^2(x) - \sin(x) - 1 = 0$ for $x$ in the interval $[0, 2\pi]$.

7. Circular Functions

For the function $y = -3\cos(2x - \frac{\pi}{2}) + 1$, determine the amplitude, period, phase shift, and vertical shift.

8. Circular Functions

The voltage in an AC circuit is given by $V(t) = 120\sin(120\pi t - \pi)$, where $t$ is time in seconds. What is the maximum voltage, the frequency (in Hz), and the first time $t > 0$ when the voltage is 60V?

9. Circular Functions

Prove the identity: $\frac{\sin(2x)}{1+\cos(2x)} = \tan(x)$.

10. Circular Functions

An object's height on a spring is given by $h(t) = 4\cos(\pi t)$. Sketch two full periods of the function, labeling the maximum and minimum heights.

11. Circular Functions

Find the exact value of $\sec(\arctan(\frac{4}{3}))$.

12. Exponential & Logarithmic Functions

Solve for $x$: $5^{2x-1} = 100$. Give your answer to four decimal places.

13. Exponential & Logarithmic Functions

The population of a town is modeled by $P(t) = 2500e^{0.025t}$, where $t$ is the number of years after 2020. In what year will the population reach 4000?

14. Exponential & Logarithmic Functions

A radioactive substance decays according to the formula $A(t) = A_0 e^{-0.04t}$, where $t$ is in years. If you start with 200 grams, how many grams will be left after 10 years? What is the half-life of this substance?

15. Exponential & Logarithmic Functions

Solve for $x$: $\log_2(x) + \log_2(x-7) = 3$.

16. Exponential & Logarithmic Functions

Express as a single logarithm: $2\ln(x) - \frac{1}{2}\ln(y) + 3\ln(z)$.

17. Exponential & Logarithmic Functions

The intensity of an earthquake is measured on the Richter scale by the formula $R = \log(\frac{I}{I_0})$. How many times more intense is an earthquake measuring 8.1 on the Richter scale than one measuring 6.1?

18. Hyperbolic Functions

Given $\sinh(x) = \frac{3}{4}$, find the exact values of $\cosh(x)$ and $\tanh(x)$ without finding $x$.

19. Hyperbolic Functions

Express $y = 4\cosh(x) - 2\sinh(x)$ in terms of exponential functions and simplify.

20. Hyperbolic Functions

Solve the equation $\tanh(x) = \frac{1}{2}$ for $x$. Give the exact answer in terms of a natural logarithm.

21. Hyperbolic Functions

Evaluate $\sinh^{-1}(2)$. Express your answer as a natural logarithm.

22. Hyperbolic Functions

A heavy, flexible cable hanging under its own weight takes the shape of a catenary, described by the equation $y = a \cosh(\frac{x}{a})$. If a cable is described by $y = 20\cosh(\frac{x}{20})$, find the height of the cable when $x=10$.

23. Mixed Applications

A damped oscillating spring's displacement is given by $d(t) = 5e^{-0.1t}\cos(2\pi t)$. Calculate the displacement at $t=1$. What function describes the damping of the oscillation over time?

24. Mixed Applications

Identify the type of function represented by the equation that models the shape of a satellite dish, which is designed to reflect signals to a single point. (Linear, Quadratic, Exponential, Hyperbolic, or Rational?)
