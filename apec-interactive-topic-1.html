Pre-Colonial Era: The Foundation of Adab and Adat

The ethical framework in this era was based on the concepts of **'Adab'** (good manners and conduct) and **'Adat'** (customs and traditions). These principles were the backbone of the value system governing traditional society and its leadership.

Colonial Era: Western Influence

The arrival of European colonizers introduced and implemented Western concepts such as **'Etika'** (Ethics), **'Etos'** (Ethos), and **'Etiket'** (Etiquette). This brought new standards of behavior, especially in formal settings like administration, attire, and speech.

Post-Colonial Era: Contemporary Identity

Contemporary Malaysian ethics are a unique blend of the traditional and the modern. It is a synthesis of the pre-colonial 'Adab' and 'Adat' with the 'Etika' and 'Etiket' introduced during the colonial era. This combination forms the distinct identity of modern Malaysian society.

Core Concepts of Ethics and Civilization

Understand the key terms that form the basis of ethical discussions in Malaysia.

Adab (Manners/Conduct)

Refers to good manners, politeness, and refined behavior. It is about conducting oneself courteously and respectfully in all situations.

Adat (Customs)

Adat Resam (Traditions): Customs and traditions practiced by a particular community or ethnic group, often related to significant life events like engagements and weddings.

Adat Istiadat (Ceremonies): Refers to official ceremonies and formal protocols that require strict adherence to established customs, such as a monarch's coronation.

Etika (Ethics)

Moral principles or values that serve as a guide for an individual's or a group's behavior. It deals with concepts of right and wrong, responsibility, and what is beneficial to society.

Etos (Ethos)

The characteristic spirit, values, and beliefs of a culture, community, or era. In the Malaysian context, it refers to the collective effort to build a harmonious and inclusive nation from diverse historical, ethnic, and cultural backgrounds.

Etiket (Etiquette)

The set of rules and conventions governing polite behavior in social or professional life. It includes protocols and procedures for formal ceremonies and interactions.

Levels of Ethical Appreciation

Ethics are formed and practiced at various levels, starting from the individual and expanding to the global society.

Individual, Family, Community

The foundational stages where ethics are first learned and instilled. An individual's character is built within the family and reinforced by the community.

Society (Local & Global)

A society with strong ethical values fosters peace and harmony. A breakdown in ethics can lead to serious social problems.

Society, Nation & Market

Ethics are crucial for the advancement of civilization. It includes promoting positive values, government efforts to improve quality of life, and an individual's contribution to the economy.
