Bahasa Melayu: Numbers, Currency & Time

Topic 1.2: A guide to using numbers, money, and time.

1. Nombor (Numbers)

Numbers in Bahasa Melayu follow a logical system. Once you learn the basics from 1-20, you can construct almost any number.

Basics (1-10)

  • 1 - Satu
  • 2 - Dua
  • 3 - Tiga
  • 4 - Empat
  • 5 - Lima
  • 6 - Enam
  • 7 - Tujuh
  • 8 - Lapan
  • 9 - Sembilan
  • 10 - Sepuluh

Teens (11-20)

  • 11 - Sebelas
  • 12 - Dua belas
  • 13 - Tiga belas
  • 14 - Empat belas
  • 15 - Lima belas
  • 16 - Enam belas
  • 17 - Tujuh belas
  • 18 - Lapan belas
  • 19 - Sembilan belas
  • 20 - Dua puluh

Larger Numbers

  • 10 - Sepuluh
  • 100 - Seratus
  • 1,000 - Seribu
  • 10,000 - Sepuluh ribu
  • 100,000 - Seratus ribu
  • 1,000,000 - Sejuta

2. Mata Wang (Currency)

The Malaysian currency is the **Ringgit (RM)** and **Sen**. There are 100 sen in 1 ringgit.

Amount Pronunciation (Sebutan)
RM 0.50 Lima puluh sen
RM 1.00 Satu ringgit
RM 10.50 Sepuluh ringgit lima puluh sen
RM 100.45 Seratus ringgit empat puluh lima sen

Note: When writing a cheque, you add **"sahaja"** (only) at the end.

3. Waktu (Time)

Parts of the Day

  • Pagi (Morning: ~1am - 11:59am)
  • Tengah Hari (Noon: 12pm - 1:59pm)
  • Petang (Afternoon: 2pm - 6:59pm)
  • Malam (Night: 7pm - 11:59pm)

How to Tell Time

Use the word "Pukul" before the hour.

  • 4:00 → Pukul empat
  • 1:15 → Pukul satu lima belas minit
  • 12:30 → Pukul dua belas tiga puluh minit

Days of the Week (Hari)

Isnin, Selasa, Rabu, Khamis, Jumaat, Sabtu, Ahad

Months of the Year (Bulan)

Januari, Februari, Mac, April, Mei, Jun, Julai, Ogos, September, Oktober, November, Disember
