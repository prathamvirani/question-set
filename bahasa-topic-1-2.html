1. Nombor (Numbers)

Understanding numbers is essential. Hereâ€™s how they are constructed in Bahasa Melayu, from basic digits to large figures.

Basics (0-10)

  • 0: Kosong
  • 1: Satu
  • 2: Dua
  • 3: Tiga
  • 4: Empat
  • 5: Lima
  • 6: Enam
  • 7: Tujuh
  • 8: Lapan
  • 9: Sembilan
  • 10: Sepuluh

Teens (11-19)

Add 'belas' to the base number.

  • 11: Sebelas
  • 12: Dua belas
  • 13: Tiga belas
  • 14: Empat belas
  • 19: Sembilan belas

Tens (20-90)

Add 'puluh' to the base number.

  • 20: Dua puluh
  • 21: Dua puluh satu
  • 30: Tiga puluh
  • 99: Sembilan puluh sembilan

Larger Numbers

Term Meaning Example (Number)
ratus hundred 101: Seratus satu
ribu thousand 1,000: Seribu
juta million 1,000,000: Sejuta

Example: 3,497,208 is "Tiga juta empat ratus sembilan puluh tujuh ribu dua ratus lapan".

2. Mata Wang (Currency)

The Malaysian currency is the **Ringgit (RM)** and **Sen**. There are 100 sen in 1 Ringgit.

Amount How to Say in Bahasa Melayu
RM 0.50 Lima puluh sen
RM 1.00 Satu ringgit
RM 10.50 Sepuluh ringgit lima puluh sen
RM 200.50 Dua ratus ringgit lima puluh sen

Note for writing cheques: Add the word "sahaja" (only) at the end. For example: "Dua ratus ringgit lima puluh sen sahaja."

3. Waktu (Time)

"Masa" is the general term for time, which includes hours, days, months, and years.

Pagi (Morning)
Tengah hari (Noon: 12pm-2pm)
Petang (Afternoon: 2pm-7pm)
Malam (Night: 7pm onwards)

Hari (Days of the Week)

  • Isnin: Monday
  • Selasa: Tuesday
  • Rabu: Wednesday
  • Khamis: Thursday
  • Jumaat: Friday
  • Sabtu: Saturday
  • Ahad: Sunday

Bulan (Months of the Year)

  • Januari: January
  • Februari: February
  • Mac: March
  • April: April
  • ...and so on.
