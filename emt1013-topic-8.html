Engineering Mathematics: Complex Numbers Test

Questions & Detailed Answers

Questions

Section A: Polar Form Calculations

1.

Given $z_1 = 5(\cos{45^\circ} + j\sin{45^\circ})$ and $z_2 = 2(\cos{15^\circ} + j\sin{15^\circ})$, calculate the product $z_1z_2$ and express it in Cartesian form ($a+jb$).

2.

Given $z_1 = 10(\cos{120^\circ} + j\sin{120^\circ})$ and $z_2 = 2(\cos{30^\circ} + j\sin{30^\circ})$, calculate the quotient $\frac{z_1}{z_2}$ and express it in Cartesian form.

3.

Evaluate the following expression and leave the answer in polar form: $$ \frac{4(\cos{80^\circ} + j\sin{80^\circ}) \times 3(\cos{20^\circ} + j\sin{20^\circ})}{6(\cos{50^\circ} + j\sin{50^\circ})} $$

4.

Use De Moivre's Theorem to find $[2(\cos{25^\circ} + j\sin{25^\circ})]^4$.

5.

Find the principal square root of $9(\cos{100^\circ} + j\sin{100^\circ})$.

6.

Convert $z_1 = 1 + j\sqrt{3}$ and $z_2 = \sqrt{3} - j$ to polar form. Then, find their product $z_1z_2$ in polar form and convert the result back to Cartesian form.

7.

Convert $z_1 = -2 + 2j$ and $z_2 = 3j$ to polar form. Then, find the quotient $\frac{z_1}{z_2}$ and express the result in Cartesian form.

8.

Evaluate the expression $\frac{(1+j)^3}{(\sqrt{3}-j)^2}$ by first converting the numerator and denominator to polar form. Leave your answer in polar form.

Section B: Roots of Complex Numbers

9.

Find all three cube roots of $z = 27(\cos{180^\circ} + j\sin{180^\circ})$.

10.

Find all four fourth roots of $z = 16$.

11.

Find the five fifth roots of $z = -32j$. State which of the roots is the principal root.

12.

Find the three cube roots of the complex number $z = -4\sqrt{2} + j4\sqrt{2}$.

13.

The cube roots of a complex number $z$ are plotted on an Argand diagram. If one root is $2(\cos{75^\circ} + j\sin{75^\circ})$, determine the other two roots.

14.

Find all the fourth roots of $z = 81(\cos{240^\circ} + j\sin{240^\circ})$ and identify the principal root.

Section C: Expansions

15.

Use De Moivre's theorem to express $\cos(5\theta)$ in terms of powers of $\cos\theta$.

16.

Use De Moivre's theorem to express $\sin(4\theta)$ in terms of powers of $\sin\theta$ and $\cos\theta$.

17.

Express $\cos^4\theta$ in terms of cosines of multiples of $\theta$.

18.

Express $\sin^5\theta$ in terms of sines of multiples of $\theta$.

19.

Express $\cos^2\theta \sin^3\theta$ in terms of sines of multiples of $\theta$.

Section D: Loci Problems

20.

If $z = x + jy$, find the Cartesian equation of the locus defined by $|z - 3| = 4$. Describe the geometric shape of the locus.

21.

If $z = x + jy$, find the Cartesian equation of the locus defined by $\text{arg}(z) = \frac{2\pi}{3}$. Describe the locus.

22.

Find the Cartesian equation of the locus of a point $z$ such that $|z - 2j| = |z + 4|$.

23.

If $z = x + jy$, show that the locus defined by $|\frac{z - 1}{z + 1}| = 3$ is a circle. Find the coordinates of its center and the value of its radius.

24.

Find the equation of the locus of $z$ if $\text{arg}(\frac{z-1}{z+j}) = \frac{\pi}{2}$.

25.

A point $z$ moves such that its distance from the point $(2,1)$ is equal to its distance from the point $(4,-3)$ in the Argand diagram. Find the Cartesian equation of the locus of $z$ and describe it geometrically.

Answers

Section A: Solutions

1. Solution

$10(\cos{60^\circ} + j\sin{60^\circ}) = 5 + j5\sqrt{3}$

2. Solution

$5(\cos{90^\circ} + j\sin{90^\circ}) = 5j$

3. Solution

$2(\cos{50^\circ} + j\sin{50^\circ})$

4. Solution

$16(\cos{100^\circ} + j\sin{100^\circ})$

5. Solution

$3(\cos{50^\circ} + j\sin{50^\circ})$

6. Solution

$z_1 = 2(\cos{60^\circ} + j\sin{60^\circ})$, $z_2 = 2(\cos{-30^\circ} + j\sin{-30^\circ})$. Product: $4(\cos{30^\circ} + j\sin{30^\circ}) = 2\sqrt{3} + 2j$.

7. Solution

$z_1 = 2\sqrt{2}(\cos{135^\circ} + j\sin{135^\circ})$, $z_2 = 3(\cos{90^\circ} + j\sin{90^\circ})$. Quotient: $\frac{2\sqrt{2}}{3}(\cos{45^\circ} + j\sin{45^\circ}) = \frac{2}{3} + \frac{2}{3}j$.

8. Solution

$\frac{2\sqrt{2}(\cos{135^\circ})}{4(\cos{-60^\circ})} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}(\cos{195^\circ} + j\sin{195^\circ})$

Section B: Solutions

9. Solution

$z_1 = 3(\cos{60^\circ} + j\sin{60^\circ})$, $z_2 = 3(\cos{180^\circ} + j\sin{180^\circ})$, $z_3 = 3(\cos{300^\circ} + j\sin{300^\circ})$

10. Solution

$z_1 = 2(\cos{0^\circ} + j\sin{0^\circ}) = 2$, $z_2 = 2(\cos{90^\circ} + j\sin{90^\circ}) = 2j$, $z_3 = 2(\cos{180^\circ} + j\sin{180^\circ}) = -2$, $z_4 = 2(\cos{270^\circ} + j\sin{270^\circ}) = -2j$

11. Solution

$z = 32(\cos{270^\circ} + j\sin{270^\circ})$. The roots are $2(\cos{(54+72k)^\circ} + j\sin{(54+72k)^\circ})$ for $k=0,1,2,3,4$. The principal root is $z_4 = 2(\cos{342^\circ} + j\sin{342^\circ})$.

12. Solution

$z = 8(\cos{135^\circ} + j\sin{135^\circ})$. The roots are $2(\cos{(45+120k)^\circ} + j\sin{(45+120k)^\circ})$ for $k=0,1,2$.

13. Solution

The roots are separated by $\frac{360^\circ}{3}=120^\circ$. The other two roots are $2(\cos{195^\circ} + j\sin{195^\circ})$ and $2(\cos{315^\circ} + j\sin{315^\circ})$.

14. Solution

The roots are $3(\cos{(60+90k)^\circ} + j\sin{(60+90k)^\circ})$ for $k=0,1,2,3$. The roots are $3\angle60^\circ, 3\angle150^\circ, 3\angle240^\circ, 3\angle330^\circ$. The principal root is $3\angle330^\circ$ as its angle ($-30^\circ$) is closest to the positive x-axis.

Section C: Solutions

15. Solution

$\cos(5\theta) = 16\cos^5\theta - 20\cos^3\theta + 5\cos\theta$

16. Solution

$\sin(4\theta) = 4\cos^3\theta\sin\theta - 4\cos\theta\sin^3\theta$

17. Solution

$\cos^4\theta = \frac{1}{8}(\cos(4\theta) + 4\cos(2\theta) + 3)$

18. Solution

$\sin^5\theta = \frac{1}{16}(\sin(5\theta) - 5\sin(3\theta) + 10\sin\theta)$

19. Solution

$\cos^2\theta \sin^3\theta = \frac{1}{16}(2\sin\theta + \sin(3\theta) - \sin(5\theta))$

Section D: Solutions

20. Solution

$(x-3)^2 + y^2 = 16$. This is a circle with center (3, 0) and radius 4.

21. Solution

$y = -\sqrt{3}x$ for $x < 0$. This is a straight line (a ray) through the origin in the second quadrant.

22. Solution

$y = -2x - 3$. This is the perpendicular bisector of the line segment joining the points $(0, 2)$ and $(-4, 0)$.

23. Solution

The locus is $8x^2 + 20x + 8y^2 + 8 = 0$, which simplifies to $(x + \frac{5}{4})^2 + y^2 = (\frac{3}{4})^2$. This is a circle with center $(-\frac{5}{4}, 0)$ and radius $\frac{3}{4}$.

24. Solution

The locus is the part of the circle $(x-\frac{1}{2})^2 + (y+\frac{1}{2})^2 = \frac{1}{2}$ that lies on the arc between the points $(1,0)$ and $(0,-1)$.

25. Solution

The locus is the perpendicular bisector of the line segment joining $(2,1)$ and $(4,-3)$. The equation is $x - 2y - 5 = 0$.
