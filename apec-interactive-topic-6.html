Historical Journey to the Constitution

The Federal Constitution is a product of a long historical process. Click on each stage to learn more.

Pre-Colonial Laws

Sophisticated legal systems like the *Hukum Kanun Melaka* existed long before colonization.

Malayan Union (1946)

A British proposal that was rejected for diminishing the Malay Rulers' sovereignty and its liberal citizenship terms.

Federation of Malaya (1948)

Restored the Rulers' positions and tightened citizenship, creating a more acceptable foundation for the nation.

Ethnic Cooperation & CLC (1949)

The Communities Liaison Committee fostered inter-ethnic cooperation by negotiating key issues like citizenship and special rights.

General Election (1955)

The Alliance Party's victory demonstrated the viability of a multi-ethnic political partnership and inclusive governance.

Reid Commission (1956)

An independent commission of legal experts drafted the Constitution after extensive consultation with over 131 groups.

"Elemen Warisan" (Heritage Elements)

These are the traditional elements preserved in the Constitution that reflect the nation's identity and form the bedrock of the social contract.

The Institution of Monarchy

Upholds the position of the Malay Rulers and the Yang di-Pertuan Agong, symbolizing unity, tradition, and continuity.

Islam as the Religion of the Federation

Article 3 establishes Islam as the official religion while guaranteeing the freedom for people of other faiths to practice their religions in peace and harmony.

Special Position of Bumiputera

Article 153 protects the special position of the Malays and natives of Sabah and Sarawak, while also safeguarding the legitimate interests of all other communities.

Malay as the National Language

Article 152 establishes the Malay language as the national language to foster unity, while protecting the right to use and learn other languages.
