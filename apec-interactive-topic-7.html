The Evolution of ICT in Malaysia

Click through the decades to see key milestones in Malaysia's technological journey.

The Dawn of Information Technology

The term "information technology" comes into use. The first commercial computer, UNIVAC 1, is developed, marking the beginning of the digital age.

Connecting Malaysia to the World

Dr. Mohamed Awang Lah pioneers the concept of connecting Malaysia to the global internet. In 1984, the first connection is established at MIMOS, and the Malaysian Computer Network (RangKoM) is set up in 1989.

Building National Infrastructure

The JARING project is launched in 1991 to create a national communication network. By 1997, the government is actively developing ICT infrastructure, including LAN, email, and web capabilities for wider use.

The Rise of the Digital Economy

The focus shifts to optimizing internet access for all. This decade sees the rapid rise of e-commerce, e-learning, and e-government, integrating ICT into the daily lives of Malaysians.

ICT as a Driver for National Unity

ICT can be a powerful tool for fostering social cohesion by connecting diverse communities and promoting shared values.

ADAM Application

An innovative application from Universiti Malaysia Pahang (UMP) that uses gamification to teach engineering students about ethnic relations, patriotism, and national identity, making learning about unity engaging.

MCMC & CMA 1998

The Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission (MCMC), established under the Communications and Multimedia Act 1998, regulates the industry to ensure it operates with transparency, fairness, and protects national interests.
