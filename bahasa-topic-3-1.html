2. Grammar: Nouns & Pronouns

Kata Nama (Nouns)

Nouns name people, places, or things. They are divided into two main types:

Kata Nama Am (Common Nouns)

General names. e.g., `universiti` (university), `bandar` (city).

Kata Nama Khas (Proper Nouns)

Specific names. e.g., `Universiti Sunway`, `Kajang`.

Kata Ganti Nama Diri (Personal Pronouns)

Person Singular (Tunggal) Plural (Jamak)
First Person `saya`, `aku` (I, me) `kita` (we - inclusive), `kami` (we - exclusive)
Second Person `awak`, `kamu`, `anda` (you) `awak semua`, `kamu semua` (all of you)
Third Person `dia`, `ia`, `beliau` (he/she) `mereka` (they, them)

3. Grammar: Basic Sentence Patterns

Malay sentences are built on a `Subject` + `Predicate` structure. Here are the most common patterns.

Pattern 1 (FN + FN): Noun + Noun
e.g., `Bapa saya` (Subject) `guru.` (Predicate) -> My father is a teacher.

Pattern 2 (FN + FA): Noun + Adjective
e.g., `Ahmad` (Subject) `kacak.` (Predicate) -> Ahmad is handsome.

Pattern 4 (FN + FSN): Noun + Prepositional Phrase
e.g., `Abang dia` (Subject) `di rumah.` (Predicate) -> His brother is at home.

4. Grammar: Prepositions (Kata Sendi Nama)

Prepositions show relationships, often indicating location, direction, or possession.

Preposition Usage Example Sentence
di in, at (location) Saya di dalam kelas. (I am in the class.)
ke to (direction) Dia pergi ke perpustakaan. (She goes to the library.)
dari from (place, time) Awak dari mana? (Where are you from?)
daripada from (person, source, comparison) Hadiah ini daripada ayah saya. (This gift is from my father.)
